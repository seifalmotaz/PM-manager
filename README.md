# Saha — Project management that actually understands you

## The problem

I'm a new project manager, and I'm struggling.

I use Trello because it's approachable, but it falls short the moment I need anything real: velocity tracking, sprint planning, capacity checks. Trello is a to-do list wearing a kanban costume — and that's not project management.

I tried Jira because that's what "real" teams use. But Jira is a cathedral built for enterprises with dedicated admins. Every view requires 40 clicks. Every feature has a 15-year legacy behind it. I spent more time configuring Jira than managing my projects.

There was nothing in between. Nothing built for people like me — new PMs who are learning the craft but need real tools, not toys.

## The reality we live in

Here's something most PM tools don't acknowledge: **we don't work at one company anymore.**

I have a day job. I also freelance after hours. These are separate worlds — separate teams, separate deadlines, separate mental spaces. But every tool forces me to create separate accounts, log out, log in, juggle browser tabs. As if my work identity is just one thing.

It's not. And my tool should know that.

## What we're building

**Saha** is a project management tool designed for people who live between Trello and Jira — and between multiple work identities.

- **One account, many worlds.** Your personal workspace, your day job, your freelance clients — all in one place. Toggle between them or see everything at once. Privacy-safe design means your workspace names never leak during screen shares.

- **Sprints that actually make sense.** Flexible durations — one week, two weeks, ten days, whatever your team runs. Real velocity tracking: planned vs. completed, with the ability to see over-velocity tasks and understand why they appeared mid-sprint. Not just a number — context.

- **Type like you think.** Quick-add a task by writing naturally: `Fix login bug p1 today sp:3 @ahmed`. The system reads your intent. Priority, date, points, assignee — all parsed inline. No form. No friction.

- **The team, not just the work.** A dedicated view for every employee shows their velocity, current tasks, sprint contributions, and capacity. Filter across projects and workspaces. Know who's overloaded before the sprint even starts.

- **Time has a home.** A global timesheet timer that auto-starts when work enters progress and auto-pauses when the board clears. Every task gets estimated hours from the PM and actual hours from the clock. See accuracy. See where the time goes.

- **Knowledge lives alongside tasks.** Write markdown notes in nested folders with tags. Project specs, meeting notes, onboarding guides — scoped to projects, visible across workspaces. Your PM brain, documented.

- **Never wonder what happened.** Every change — every status move, every reassignment, every description edit — is logged with who did it and when. Comments keep the conversation going. Activity feeds tell the story.

- **Find anything, instantly.** `Cmd+K` opens a global search across tasks, projects, and files. Keyboard-driven. Zero clicks to get where you need to go.

## Why now?

Most PM tools were built for the era when you had one job, one team, one tool.

That world is gone.

We work across contexts. We manage capacity, not just cards. We need velocity, not just checkboxes. And the tools that exist are either too simple to be useful or too complex to be usable.

Saha bridges the gap — built by someone who lives in it, for people who live in it too.

The AI-native layer (MCP) comes next. But first: a PM tool that actually fits the modern worker.
